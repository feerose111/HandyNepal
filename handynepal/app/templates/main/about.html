About HandyNepal

Connecting global customers with authentic Nepalese craftsmanship

Our Story

HandyNepal was born from a passion for preserving Nepal's rich cultural heritage and supporting the livelihoods of skilled artisans across the country.

Founded in 2018 by Nepalese entrepreneurs who witnessed firsthand the challenges faced by traditional craftspeople in a rapidly modernizing world, our mission was clear: create a bridge between these talented artisans and global customers who appreciate authentic, handcrafted treasures.

What began as a small collection of wooden crafts from Bhaktapur has grown into a comprehensive marketplace featuring hundreds of handcrafted items from over 50 artisans across Nepal. Each piece in our collection tells a story of cultural heritage, exceptional skill, and artistic tradition.

Our Mission & Values

What drives us every day

Artisan Empowerment

We believe in fair compensation and sustainable livelihoods for artisans. By eliminating middlemen and working directly with craftspeople, we ensure they receive the majority of proceeds from each sale.

Cultural Preservation

We're committed to preserving traditional Nepalese crafts that have been passed down through generations. By creating a market for these items, we help ensure these ancient techniques don't disappear.

Global Connection

We connect cultures by bringing authentic Nepalese craftsmanship to homes around the world, fostering appreciation for Nepal's rich artistic heritage and the stories behind each handcrafted piece.

Our Impact

Making a difference in artisan communities

Sustainable Livelihoods

We've helped over 50 artisan families achieve stable incomes through fair trade practices, allowing them to improve their living conditions and invest in their children's education.

Education Initiatives

A portion of every purchase funds educational programs for artisans' children, with over 30 students currently receiving support for school supplies and tuition.

Craft Preservation

We've established apprenticeship programs in 5 villages, where master artisans train the next generation in traditional techniques that might otherwise be lost.
